There are $N$ frogs numbered from $1\dots N$. Today, they are going to race each other in the National Frog Olympics competition!

The race will be on an $N$ x $M$ grid of lily pads. Each of the $M$ columns of the grid will hold $N$ lilypads in a permutation of integers from $1\dots N$.

At the beginning of the race, each frog is situated in the leftmost column, at the lilypad corresponding to their number. The frogs will attempt to reach the rightmost lilypad with their number as fast as possible.

In one jump, a frog will move to the next lilypad on the right with their number. The time used to complete the jump will be the absolute difference in vertical distance between the pads.

Output how long it will take each frog to finish the race.

Input

The first line contains two integers $N$ and $M$ $(1 \le N \le 10^3, 1 \le M \le 10^3)$

The next $N$ lines contain $M$ integers: the $N$ by $M$ grid which specifies the number of each lilypad.

Output

Output $N$ lines, where the $i$th line contains the time it took the $i$th frog to finish the race.

Sample Input
3 4 1 3 1 2 2 1 3 3 3 2 2 1 Sample Output
4 3 3

Note


The third frog took $2$ seconds to jump from the first to the second column, $1$ second to jump from the second to the third column, and $0$ seconds to jump from the third to the fourth column.

Subtask 1: $N,M \le 10^2$
Subtask 2: No Additional Constraints
