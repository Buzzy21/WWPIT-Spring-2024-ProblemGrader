Rishikesh is taking his AP Physics test and he needs help with a problem on it.

There is an atom with a bunch of electrons, each with distinct $x$ and $y$ coordinates. The flux of a quadrilateral is defined to be the number of electrons inside of it. Find the sum of flux of every convex quadrilateral whose corners are all electrons.

Particles on the edge of a quadrilateral do not count.

Input

The first line is an integer $N$ $(5 \le N \le 150)$.

The next $N$ lines are 2 integers $x$, $y$ $(-10^4 \le x,y \le 10^4)$. All coordinates are guaranteed to be distinct.

Output

Output 1 integer, the sum of flux of every quadruplet of electrons.

Sample Input
10 17 -58 72 -93 3 -4 37 81 8 79 98 -84 30 36 -71 -16 -16 -58 76 -57 Sample Output
159

Note

Consider the following quadrilateral:

$(17,-58)$
$(37,81)$
$(8,79)$
$(-16,-58)$

It contains two points $(30,36)$ and $(3,-4)$. The sum of all these counts for every quadrilateral is $111$.

Subtask 1: $N \le 10$
Subtask 2: $N \le 50$
Subtask 3: No additional constraints
