Jie is taking his AP Chemistry test, and he needs your help with a problem.

You are given a molecular structure (graph) with $N$ atoms (nodes) and $M$ bonds (edges). Each bond is only stable until time $t$.

The entire graph is stable as long as there exists at least 2 distinct paths between every pair of nodes. Find the maximum time that the graph is stable for.

A path is only valid if they contain no duplicate edges or nodes.

2 paths are distinct if they have a different length or there is a node in one path at a specific point that isn't in the other path at that point

Input

The first line contains 2 integers $N$ and $M$, $(1 \le N,M \le 10^5)$

The next $M$ lines contain 3 integers, $a$, $b$, and $t$ $(1 \le a,b \le 10^5)$ $(1 \le t \le 10^9)$ representing that an edge from $a$ to $b$ is stable for $t$ time.

The graph is guaranteed to have no multi-edges or self-loops.

Output

Output 1 integer, the maximum amount of time the graph is stable for.

Sample Input
6 7 1 2 3 5 1 9 3 4 1000000000 2 3 4 4 5 7 5 6 10 6 3 10 Sample Output
3

Note

The graph is a cycle from 1 $\rightarrow$ 2 $\rightarrow$ 3 $\rightarrow$ 4 $\rightarrow$ 5 and back to 1. If any edge is deleted, the graph becomes a line and there is only 1 path between any pair of nodes. The first edge to be deleted is deleted at time 3.

Subtask 1: $N,M \le 10$
Subtask 2: $N,M \le 10^3$
Subtask 3: No additional constraints
