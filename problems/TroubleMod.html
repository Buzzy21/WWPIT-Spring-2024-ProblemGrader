Maitian has an array $a$ of length $N$. He has been having trouble with mod. To practice his modular skills, he is trying to solve the following problem:

Let $s$ be a subsequence of $a$. Let $p$ be maximum value of the $gcf(s_1,s_2,...)\cdot lcm(s_1,s_2,...)$. Find $p \bmod 10^9+7$.

A sequence $b$ is a subsequence of $a$ if $b$ can be obtained from $a$ by the deletion of several (possibly, zero or all) elements, without changing the order of the remaining elements. For example, $[5,2,3]$ is a subsequence of $[1,5,7,8,2,4,3]$.

The GCF and LCM of a single number is itself.

Input

The first line contains 1 integer $N$ $(1 \le N \le 5\cdot 10^4)$.

The next line contains the $N$ integers of $a$ $(1 \le a_i \le 5\cdot 10^4)$.

Output

Output $p \bmod 10^9+7$ on a single line.

Sample Input
10 8 1 2 7 12 9 13 14 19 17
Sample Output
2116296

Note

The optimal solution is to take the entire array as a subset, making the GCD $1$ and LCM $2116296$. It can be proven that this is the optimal solution.

Subtask 1: $N \le 100$
Subtask 2: No additional constraints
