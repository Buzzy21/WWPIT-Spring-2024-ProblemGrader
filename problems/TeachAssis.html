Maitian has just become a teacher's assistant. The classrooms of HSS are represented by $N$ classrooms and $N-1$ corridors, such that you can get from any classroom to any other classroom through corridors. Each corridor has a different length.

There are $K$ teachers he must help, all at different classrooms.

Classroom $1$ is the entrance. Maitian must start at the entrance of the school, go to each classroom that a teacher needs help with in any order, and exit back at the entrance of the school.

Please find the minimum total length he must walk to fulfill the needs of all the teachers.

Input

The first line contains two integers $N$, $K$ $(1 \le N \le 10^5, 1 \le K \le N)$.

The next $N-1$ lines each contain three integers, $a$, $b$, and $w$ $(1 \le a,b \le N, 1 \le w \le 10^9)$ representing a corridor between classrooms $a$ and $b$ of length $w$

The next line contains $K$ distinct classrooms $c_1,c_2,...,c_K$ $(1 \le c_i \le N)$, the classrooms that a teacher needs help in.

Output

Print the minimum total distance Maitian must walk.

Sample Input
4 2 1 2 2 2 3 3 2 4 1 3 4 Sample Output
12

Note

Maitian must visit both classrooms 3 and 4 to help teachers. Here is his path.
$1 \rightarrow 2$ length: 2
$2 \rightarrow 3$ length: 3
$3 \rightarrow 2$ length: 3
$2 \rightarrow 4$ length: 1
$4 \rightarrow 2$ length: 1
$2 \rightarrow 1$ length: 2
The total length Maitian must walk is $12$.

Subtask 1: $N \le 10^3, K \le 20$
Subtask 2: $N \le 10^3$
Subtask 3: No additional constraints
